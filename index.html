Paid to Play: A Breakdown of the Top 100 Twitch Streamers

By Alex Cohen, Joseph Hier, Hollis Lehv, and Matteo Sandrin


Can you really make thousands playing video games from your parents' basement? Some are, and they're doing it on a site called Twitch. Started in 2011, Twitch is a streaming service with over 140 million active users per month, accruing an estimated $1.54 billion annual revenue. Though the most known for live game streaming, Twitch has become a community of its own with users watching their favorite streamers do anything from cook to dance to work out.

Ludwig Ahgren started streaming on Twitch in May, 2018. 3 years later, he's the most subscribed to streamer on twitch with 246611 subscribers. Each pays him $5 a month to watch him play video games, taste test cheeses, or try on costumes with his girlfriend.

Ahgren's subscriber count skyrocked in early March 2021 when he began a "subathon", an event in which a streamer performs actions or offers rewards in return for subscriptions. Ahgren's reward was to stay live a few seconds longer for every subscriber. At the time of writing, he has been streaming non-stop for 28 days.

Mr. Ahgren isn't alone in the amount of money he has made streaming. Streamers can make tens of thousands of dollars per month. Mr. Ahgren has revealed he made at least $100,000 over the course of his 28 day subathon.

So who exactly are these content creaters making boatloads of money working at home playing video games?

Top 100 Streamers are overwhelmingly male

Like 98% of the top 100 streamers on Twitch, Mr. Ahgren is male. Despite 35% of viewers on Twitch being female, the top eschelon of streamers remain overwhelmingly male.

One of the rare female streamers in the top 100 is Imane Anys or Pokimane. Anys began streaming in 2013 playing games like League of Legends, and Fortnite.

In terms of her age, she's no anomaly. Like 36% of top 100 streamers she's between 23 and 27 years old (24).

Some question the lasting potential of Twitch. Is a business model based around voluntary subscriptions and donations sustainable? Will the lack of diversity on Twitch be its downfall? Will twitch expand to a wider audience beyond gaming? We invite you to think about these questions as you explore the backgrounds, personalities, and stories of the Twitch elite.

Who's your (data) source?

The data for this visualization was gathered from sullygnome.com, which is a statistics and analytics service for Twitch. SullyGnome uses the Twitch API to collect information on every Twitch streamer with 3 or more viewers, at 15-minute intervals. This data is then aggregated and displayed on the website. The historical data provided by SullyGnome goes as far back as 2015.


We wrote a collection of Python scripts to scrape ranking, viewer, follower and subscriber historical data from SullyGnome, for each of the top 100 Twitch streamers. These scripts were run in a staggered fashion over the course of a 24-hour window, with the aim of reducing load on SullyGnomeâ€™s servers.
